Welcome to SIH1545 By Team KanyaRaasi

Our project focuses on developing a non-electrical device for tracking the movement of the sun to increase the efficiency of solar panels. Inspired by artificial heliotropism, similar to the behavior of a sunflower, we use a Liquid Crystal Polymer to allow solar panels to bend towards sunlight. This polymer compresses under sunlight and reverts when no light is present, making the solar panels track the sun naturally.

Technical Approach: Our approach incorporates various technologies, including Spring Technology, Elastomer Technology, and Liquid Crystal Polymer, to make the movement efficient and non-electrical.

Impact & Benefits: By using this hybrid model, solar panels can reach up to 43% solar energy utilization, and this can be applied even in regions with extended sunlight exposure like the Arctic Circle.
